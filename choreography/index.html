Ideas Move: Choreography

Ava’s choreography amplifies sensations to question how fully experiencing an external quality enables internal truths to emerge.

"It Doesn’t Hurt Me When." Performed by Ava Louise Untermyer. Score composed by Ava Louise Untermyer, using interviews of those who have had experience with disability. Disability and Dance Open Showing, 2011.

"It Doesn’t Hurt Me When"

Choreographed as a part of a "Disability in Dance and Literature" Project with disability activist Theri Pickens. The piece weaves together the difficulties and liberations that emerge from moving under limitations to present limitation as a quintessentially human experience.

"He Hardly Lets Me Stir"

Performed by Claudia Cook, Katelyn Muir, Smanatha Hill and Anya Leyhe. Musical score composed by Adrian Untermyer. In the Works Dance Concert, 2010. "He Hardly Lets Me Stir" looms with crawling women, screeching babies, and slender, long white gloves to evoke eerily perfected domestic life. The piece is choreographed in response to the short story "The Yellow Wall Paper" written by Charlotte Perkins Gillman. Ava has additionally choreographed for musical theatre productions, including "Real Dorms" (2011) and "Meeting Marlene" (2009).
