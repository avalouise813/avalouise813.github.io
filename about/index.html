About Ava Louise Untermyer

Ava Louise Untermyer is a New York based modern dance artist who moves to stimulate livelihood in others. She currently works as a freelance performer, dancing for choreographers such as Kayt MacMaster, Hazel Lever and Eli Tamondong. Ava earned honors for her B.A. in Dance Movement Analysis and English & World Literature at Pitzer College of the Claremont College Consortium. While in school, Ava cultivated her individual voice as an artist who colors organic movements with an expansive sense of energy.

She performed the original works of internationally acclaimed modern dance artists, including Dai Jian, John Pennington, Jessica Harris, Martha Graham, Meg Jolley and Peter Pucci. Ava was awarded the Virginia Princehouse Allen Prize for hard work, talent and perseverance in dance at the Claremont Colleges. In 2013 Ava attended the Bates Dance Festival where she performed an original work by Bridgeman & Packer and in 2012 she attended the American Dance Festival where she performed works by William Forsythe, Rainey Lacey and Victoria Woolfe. Ava additionally studied at the Jose Limon Dance Institute under Carla Maxwell, Risa Steinburg and Alan Danielson where she performed classic modern dance works, including "Water Study", by Doris Humphrey and "Choreographic Offering", by Jose Limon.

In addition to dancing, Ava offers private lessons in the Gyrotonic (R) Expansion Method to further promote physical enlivenment. Throughout all dimensions of her movement artistry, Ava evokes the the energy in motion.
